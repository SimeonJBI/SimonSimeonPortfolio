Structured cabling and installation system camera surveillance

This Services refers to the design, installation, and maintenance of a network's physical infrastructure. This includes the cables, connectors, and other components that connect devices such as computers, servers, and routers. A structured cabling system is designed to be flexible, scalable, and easy to troubleshoot, making it easy to add, move, or change devices on the network. The most common types of structured cabling include Copper (Cat5, Cat6, etc), Fiber optic and Wireless. The standard for structured cabling is TIA/EIA-568-B which defines the performance characteristics, and installation requirements for copper and fiber optic cabling systems.

For the installation system camera surveillance my job involves structured cabling, setting up and installing security camera systems in a specific location. This includes tasks such as:

Key Responsibilities

  • Planning the camera layout and determining the best locations for cameras to maximize coverage and visibility.
  • Running cables and installing cameras, including both fixed and PTZ (pan-tilt-zoom) cameras.
  • Configuring the cameras and associated equipment, such as digital video recorders (DVRs) or network video recorders (NVRs).
  • Testing the cameras to ensure they are working properly and that the video feeds are clear and of good quality.
  • Setting up remote access and/or cloud storage for the cameras if needed.
  • Training the end-users on how to use and maintain the cameras.

Qualifications

  • Specific knowledge about the different types of cameras and equipment
  • Experience with electrical wiring and networking.

Network Monitor with PRTG and Solarwinds

As a Network Monitor my responsibility is for monitoring and maintaining the performance and security of an organization's computer network. This includes monitoring network traffic, identifying and resolving issues, and implementing security measures to protect against cyber threats. I may also be responsible for configuring and maintaining network equipment, such as routers and switches, as well as troubleshooting network problems and providing technical support to users. In addition, I may work with other IT staff to plan and implement network upgrades and improvements. I have Strong knowledge of networking protocols and experience with network monitoring tools

Key Responsibilities

  • Installing, configuring, and maintaining network monitoring software such as PRTG and SolarWinds.
  • Setting up sensors and monitors to track network performance metrics such as bandwidth usage, response time, and uptime.
  • Creating alerts and notifications to notify IT staff of any issues or outages.
  • Troubleshooting and resolving network problems identified by PRTG and SolarWinds monitoring software.
  • Collaborating with IT teams to ensure that all network devices and services are properly configured and monitored.
  • Keeping current with new features and updates to PRTG and SolarWinds, and making recommendations for upgrades and enhancements.
  • Creating and maintaining detailed documentation of network topology, devices, and monitoring configurations.
  • Helping to identify and mitigate security threats to the network through the use of PRTG and SolarWinds.
  • Providing training and support to other IT staff on the use of PRTG and SolarWinds.
  • Providing regular reports on network performance and availability.

Network administration

As a network administrator I'm responsible for the day-to-day operation and maintenance of an organization's computer network. This includes configuring, managing, and monitoring network devices such as routers, switches, and firewalls, as well as ensuring the network is secure and running efficiently

Key Responsibilities

  • Configuring and maintaining network hardware such as routers, switches, and firewalls.
  • Managing and monitoring network performance to ensure it is running efficiently and effectively.
  • Implementing and maintaining network security measures such as firewalls and intrusion detection systems.
  • Troubleshooting and resolving network issues as they arise.
  • Managing and maintaining the organization's telecommunication systems, such as VoIP and VPNs.
  • Planning and implementing new network projects, such as network expansions or upgrades.
  • Managing and maintaining the organization's wireless network infrastructure.
  • Creating and maintaining documentation for the network, including diagrams, configuration files, and procedures.
  • Managing and maintaining the organization's DNS and DHCP servers.
  • Managing and maintaining the organization's virtualized environment.
  • Staying current with new network technologies and trends, and researching new networking solutions to improve the organization's network infrastructure.
  • Managing user accounts and permissions, and ensuring compliance with organization's policies and regulations
  • Collaborating with other IT staff, including system administrators and security personnel, to ensure the network is running smoothly and securely.

In general list and tasks may vary depending on the size and type of organization and the specific needs of the network infrastructure.
